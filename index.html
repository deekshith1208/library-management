About CCL

City Central Library has been started on January 25, 2020 by Mr.P. Durai Selvan IAS.CCL has seven floors with six lakhs books covering all subjects.The reading hall has been equiped with AC.Each floor has been specialized for particular group of subject.We have beverage and snackes counters to serve at your table.CCL has digital library providing E-books,E-Audio books,E-Megazines, CCL has a special section for braille books and digital braille section,Net browsing section in each floor.We have the borrowing option for membership users.In CCL 1000 users can utilize at a time.CCL has built up in 10 acres with ample vehicle parking.CCL has a Conference Hall consists of 550 seatings at the seventh floor.

The library serves as a perfect reference point for academicians, students of Tamil history and culture, political speechwriters, and research scholars from foreign universities who are working on subjects related to South Asia and Southern India. Plus, in order to support and encourage researchers and scholars, there is no fee charged; the library is free for reference. Lending of books is not allowed!

There are books and categories to satisfy every reader – from biographies and novels to travelogues, folk literature, astrology, philosophy, history, culture and more. It’s a three-storey library with walls stacked with books from floor to ceiling. Some of the rare books include Veeramamunivar’s ‘Sathur Agarathy‘ (1842), a hand-written Tamil-Latin Dictionary (1867), French-Tamil Dictionary (1855), Swadeshi Geethangal (1908) by Subramaniya Bharathi, Bharathidasan collection of poems (1930), and several Tamil literary magazines from 1920 to 2010. The rarity of books, as well as Mr. Krishnamurthy’s enthusiasm to personally introduce readers to the rare gems is what makes book lovers flock to this private library.
